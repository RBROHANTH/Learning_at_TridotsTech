Resistor
A component that opposes the flow of electric current, used to limit current and divide voltages in a circuit. Its resistance is measured in Ohms (Ω).

★★★★☆

Capacitor
A device that stores and releases electrical energy within an electric field. It is commonly used for smoothing out power supply fluctuations and in timing circuits

★★★★☆

Inductor
A passive coil that stores energy in a magnetic field when electric current flows through it. It resists sudden changes in current and is widely used for filtering noise in power supplies

★★★★☆

Diode
: A semiconductor device that acts as a one-way valve for electric current. It allows current to flow in only one direction, which makes it essential for converting Alternating Current (AC) to Direct Current (DC).

★★★★☆

Transistor
A versatile semiconductor device used to either amplify electronic signals or act as a fast switch. Transistors are the building blocks of modern computing devices and microprocessors.

★★★★☆

Transformer
An electromagnetic device that transfers electrical energy from one circuit to another while changing the voltage levels (stepping up or stepping down the voltage). It generally operates only in AC circuits.

★★★★☆
